90 # ps "-o" field names differ wildly between platforms.
91 # "comm" means "only executable name" but is not available on BSD platforms.
92 # "args" means "command with all its arguments", and is also not available
93 # on BSD platforms.
94 # "command" is like "args" on most platforms, but like "comm" on AIX,
95 # and not available on SUNOS.
96 # so for the executable name we can use "comm" on Solaris and split "command"
97 # on other platforms.
98 # to get the cmdline (with args) we have to use "args" on AIX and
99 # Solaris, and can use "command" on all others.
